RFID Blocking

The radiofrequency identifying tags present in credit and debit cards can be used by card scanners for stealing sensitive data in the name of quick contactless payments.

RFID blocking refers to the process of identifying and restricting unauthorized scanning of cards, passports, and other sensitive documents.

Status: RFID Blocker OFF

What is RFID?

RFID stands for Radio Frequency Identification. It's a technology that uses electromagnetic fields to identify and track tags attached to objects, such as credit cards, passports, and ID cards.

How Does RFID Work?

  • RFID tags contain a small chip and antenna.
  • A scanner emits radio waves which power the tag and read its data.
  • This allows contactless communication between the card and the reader.

Security Issues with RFID

  • Skimming: Attackers can steal card data without touching it.
  • Eavesdropping: Sensitive data can be intercepted during transmission.
  • Cloning: Cloned RFID tags can be used to impersonate your card or ID.

How RFID Blocking Helps

  • Blocks radio signals from reaching your cards.
  • Prevents unauthorized scanning in public places.
  • Ensures your data remains secure from hidden scanners.
